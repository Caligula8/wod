The Office of National Drug Control Policy truly believes, and is wrong in doing so, that “we can reduce drug use without compromising american ideals if we maintain adequate resolve.” In this, the office claims that “The overall strategy of prohibition in the war on drugs is necessary and a largely successful approach”(Thompson 40). Drug abusers empties the moral and physical strength out of the country, they even go as far as to make the claim, “every drug user gives up their ability to act and think rationally and his potential for a full productive life” (Thompson 41). Illegal drugs are the reason behind the majority of the crime in this countries cities, as well as costs the country over $67 billion in criminal, health, and social costs each year. The Office of National Drug Control Policy believes that these cost would have been much greater if not for the government’s ‘heroic’ efforts. The ONDCP even acknowledges the misleadingness of the term ‘War on Drugs’; they said themselves the moment the declare themselves victorious with the way they are fighting the war, widespread drug abuse will rise again.
